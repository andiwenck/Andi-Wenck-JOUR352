More About Me

I'm 1/3 farm-girl, 1/3 student-athlete and 1/3 storyteller.

I know that sounds like an odd combination, but I promise you I'm normal... sorta. I grew up on a farm in Wisconsin, hauling hay, raising cattle and doing chores. Growing up on a farm has taught me a lot...
but let's talk sports.

Hello Again!

As a student-athlete, I’ve always viewed sports as a practice session for life. And now that my practice session is over, I’d like to share with you some of the ways in which sports have helped prepare me for life:

I learned that attitude is everything. I learned that self-discipline and hard work generally produce positive results. I learned that being a captain does not mean people will follow your directions–it means you have an opportunity to earn people’s respect and serve others. I learned that encouragement often goes further than criticism. And as a goalkeeper, I learned that you cannot win the game by yourself.

Like athletics, life is a team sport. If you train to be your personal best, encourage your teammates to be their best and leave everything out on the field–chances are together, you and your team, will be successful.
